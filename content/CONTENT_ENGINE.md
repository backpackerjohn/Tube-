# Content Engine

## The repeatable unit

Every long-form upload is a **decision file**, not a generic explainer. It begins with a buyer decision and ends with a conditional action.

### Episode brief template

1. **Decision:** What must the viewer decide?
2. **Buyer/use case:** Who is this for and what changes the answer?
3. **Search evidence:** vidIQ cluster and YouTube API sample.
4. **Official evidence:** tax, safety, charging or government data.
5. **Manufacturer evidence:** exact warranty/manual terms for the relevant model year.
6. **Market evidence:** dated listings, depreciation or ownership-cost inputs.
7. **Unknowns:** what requires the VIN, physical inspection, diagnostic access or a local quote.
8. **Original tool:** scorecard, calculator, matrix, map, timeline or checklist.
9. **Verdict:** Buy / Wait / Walk, with conditions.
10. **Next decision:** playlist bridge.

## Content flywheel

```
Viewer question
   ↓
Search/API validation
   ↓
Official + manufacturer evidence
   ↓
Original decision tool
   ↓
Long-form episode
   ↓
Comments categorized by model/problem
   ↓
Next buyer file + annual refresh
```

The flywheel compounds research. A battery-warranty source gathered for one model feeds the warranty playlist and future model comparisons, while each video remains substantively different.

## Six series

### 1. Buyer File

**Promise:** “Everything that changes the decision on this model/year.”

Repeatable segments:

- why it is on used lots now;
- battery system and available evidence;
- charging fit;
- warranty clock;
- recall/VIN gate;
- hidden-cost scenario;
- who should buy and who should walk.

Topic depth: each major model × model-year changes × trim/battery variants × annual market refresh.

### 2. Battery Truth

**Promise:** “Separate useful battery clues from false certainty.”

Topic bank:

- state of health versus displayed range;
- degradation versus failure;
- replacement history;
- warranty threshold versus usable range;
- cold/heat effects;
- fast-charging myths;
- LFP versus nickel-based buyer implications;
- diagnostic report interpretation;
- what an OBD tool can and cannot prove;
- preconditioning and charging curve basics.

### 3. Charging Reality

**Promise:** “Prove the car fits the viewer’s actual charging life.”

Topic bank:

- apartment test;
- Level 1 viability;
- workplace charging dependence;
- road-trip route buffer;
- connector compatibility;
- public-charging price scenarios;
- cold-weather margin;
- towing/load penalty;
- charger-install quote checklist;
- backup plan when a favorite station is offline.

DOE’s buyer framework supplies the baseline: daily mileage, charging access, route compatibility and climate/load must be evaluated together. [S02]

### 4. Money File

**Promise:** “Calculate the ownership decision the listing does not show.”

Topic bank:

- tax-credit expiry and state/utility alternatives;
- depreciation versus capability;
- insurance quote protocol;
- tire replacement scenario;
- registration and local fees;
- charging at home versus public-only;
- financing term risk on rapidly depreciating cars;
- extended-warranty decision questions;
- lease-return inventory;
- keep versus sell.

### 5. Listing Autopsy

**Promise:** “Verify every claim that can be verified before contacting the seller.”

Segments:

- decode listing language;
- identify trim/battery ambiguity;
- compare original specification with current claim;
- run general recall search, then explain VIN requirement;
- estimate warranty remaining;
- calculate price after fees;
- generate seven seller questions;
- classify evidence gaps.

Listings are captured with date and location. Personal information is removed. The channel does not accuse a seller of deception without proof.

### 6. Policy & Market Update

**Promise:** “Only changes that alter a buyer decision.”

A current event qualifies only when it changes one of:

- purchase price;
- charging access/cost;
- warranty/recall exposure;
- eligible features;
- insurance/registration;
- resale/depreciation;
- model availability.

This avoids becoming a daily EV-news channel.

## Topic scoring before greenlight

| Factor | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Decision urgency | curiosity only | useful someday | active purchase/cost risk |
| Demand proof | no signal | one source | vidIQ + API or official change |
| Evidence access | mostly anecdotal | mixed | official/manufacturer + market |
| Original tool | none | checklist | calculator/matrix/score |
| Evergreen value | under 30 days | annual | 18+ months with refresh path |
| Brand fit | adjacent | one pillar | directly answers used-EV decision |

Greenlight at **9/12** or higher. A lower-scoring video requires a written strategic reason.

## 100-topic depth map

A sustainable library is available without stretching the thesis:

- 30 model/year buyer files;
- 15 battery-health and warranty topics;
- 15 charging-fit topics;
- 15 ownership-cost and depreciation topics;
- 10 recall/VIN/service-history topics;
- 10 listing autopsies;
- 5 major policy/market updates.

This is a planning allocation, not a factual forecast.

## Packaging system

### Title formulas

- `Used [Model]: [Number] Checks Before You Pay`
- `The [Component/Policy] Changed—What Used EV Buyers Do Now`
- `Why [Common Clue] Does Not Prove [Buyer Assumption]`
- `Can You Own a Used EV Without [Resource]? Run This Test`
- `[Model] at $[Price]: Bargain, Warning or Wrong Fit?`
- `The [Record/Document] Check That Can Stop a Bad Deal`

### Hook formulas

- **Cost reversal:** “The low price is not the bargain. The remaining capability per dollar is.”
- **Evidence correction:** “This dashboard number looks like proof. It is only a clue.”
- **Clock:** “A warranty can be worth thousands, but only if you know which clock is actually running.”
- **Fit:** “A 250-mile EV can be a worse choice than a 150-mile EV when charging access changes.”
- **Policy reset:** “A benefit repeated in thousands of older videos no longer applies to a purchase made today.”

### Closing bridge

Never end with a generic request alone. End with the next decision:

> “Now that you know how to screen the battery, the next failure point is charging fit. Run the five-minute no-home-charger test next.”

## Audience research system

Comments are tagged in a simple backlog:

- model/year request;
- battery question;
- charging situation;
- warranty/recall;
- price/listing;
- keep/sell;
- correction/source challenge.

A topic receives a viewer-demand boost when:

- five independent comments ask the same decision;
- a comment contains a real deadline (“buying this weekend”);
- the question reveals a missing step in an existing playlist.

Comments are leads, not facts. Any claim still requires independent verification.

## Refresh system

Each video has a refresh class:

- **A — policy/price:** review every 30 days.
- **B — model/warranty/recall:** review every 90 days.
- **C — principles:** review every 12 months.

Refresh actions:

1. update description source links and date;
2. pin a correction/update comment;
3. replace downloadable checklist if needed;
4. produce a new edition when the decision materially changes;
5. never silently delete an incorrect historical claim.

## Shorts strategy

Shorts are optional derivatives with independent value:

- one misconception correction;
- one checklist item;
- one verified policy change;
- one chart explained in under 45 seconds.

Do not cut a random dramatic moment. Do not build a clip-compilation engine. A Short must contain an original narration, conclusion and source label, consistent with YouTube’s originality requirements. [S05]

## Newsletter/download extension

The highest-value lead magnet is the **Used EV Evidence Sheet**, a one-page copyable checklist with:

- VIN and recall status;
- warranty dates/mileage;
- battery evidence supplied;
- charging access;
- insurance quote;
- tire condition;
- out-the-door price;
- unknowns;
- buyer decision.

The package does not assume an email platform or publish the sheet. The structure can be created from the scorecard when the channel launches.

## Content quality gate

An episode does not enter editing until:

- [ ] at least one official/primary source is present;
- [ ] all model-specific claims match the relevant year/trim or are labeled uncertain;
- [ ] the “unknown” section is written;
- [ ] the original tool is complete;
- [ ] sponsor/affiliate mentions are removed from the verdict logic;
- [ ] high-voltage DIY or unsafe guidance is absent;
- [ ] title and thumbnail reflect the actual conclusion;
- [ ] source sheet has last-verified dates;
- [ ] the next-playlist bridge is chosen.
