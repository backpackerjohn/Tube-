# Candidate Opportunity Tournament

## Method

Ten channel ideas were evaluated before a winner was selected. The score is a **strategy model**, not an empirical forecast. It forces explicit tradeoffs and prevents one attractive keyword from becoming a channel thesis.

Weights:

| Criterion | Weight | What earns a high score |
|---|---:|---|
| Search demand | 20% | Multiple connected queries, not one keyword |
| Recent viewing proof | 20% | Several recent videos across different creators attract meaningful views |
| Attainable competition | 15% | Search results are not monopolized by giant incumbents; smaller channels can break out |
| Faceless/asset fit | 15% | Original value can be made without filming a presenter, location or repair |
| Commercial value | 12% | Audience is near a purchase or costly decision; relevant advertiser/sponsor categories exist |
| Topic depth | 10% | At least 100 credible episodes without drifting from the promise |
| Production simplicity/safety | 8% | Research and editing are repeatable; low physical, legal and medical risk |

Each criterion was scored 1–10. Weighted totals are out of 100. Source IDs resolve in [SOURCE_LOG.md](SOURCE_LOG.md).

## Candidate dossiers

### 1. Used-EV buyer intelligence — selected

**Demand evidence.** vidIQ surfaced a cluster rather than a single term: “tesla battery health check,” “used tesla buying guide,” “ev buying guide,” “buying used ev,” and “ev battery degradation,” generally with lower connected competition scores than broad EV terms. [V01]

**Viewing evidence.** Five recent used-EV or used-Tesla buyer videos in the API sample drew 257,224 to 469,001 views, with hundreds of comments each. [Y01–Y05]

**Market context.** Reuters reported 378,140 U.S. used-EV sales in 2025, roughly 35% above the prior year, and a further 21% year-over-year rise in January 2026. [S07] The IRS’s post-September-2025 credit change creates confusion that requires current explanation. [S01]

**Faceless proof.** Connected channel research found multiple faceless automotive ownership/buying channels with meaningful audiences, including Japanese Motors, Everything Lexus, Everything Hyundai, Auto Gear Shift and CarLandTV. Their presence proves the format can work without an on-camera host; their broad positioning leaves room for a used-EV-only evidence system.

**Production fit.** The core assets are public documents, warranty language, VIN recall results, cost models, maps, annotated listings and original scorecards. No driving footage is necessary.

**Why it is underserved.** This is an inference from the evidence: search demand and viewing are strong, but many results are personal ownership stories, general reviews or broad EV news. A channel that applies the same auditable inspection framework to every model is more specific and defensible.

### 2. Home Wi-Fi diagnosis

**For:** Exceptional recent viewing and clear searchable problems. The sample included 441,929 to 1.31 million views. [W01–W05] Screen recordings, router diagrams and animations fit a faceless workflow.

**Against:** Search demand fragments by operating system, router, internet provider and firmware version. Large technology incumbents dominate broader topics. Hardware access may still be needed to prove fixes. The content can become an endless support desk with weak subscriber cohesion.

**Decision:** Strong reserve niche, but weaker repeatable audience identity than “used-EV buyer.”

### 3. Scam prevention and fraud defense

**For:** Broad public need and multiple keyword clusters. [V02] vidIQ identified successful faceless channels, including a breakout Shorts channel.

**Against:** The strongest breakout pattern leaned on clips and caught-on-camera moments, creating copyright and reused-content pressure. Generic educational API results were less convincing than the keyword metrics. Scams evolve rapidly and mistakes can cause harm. Sponsor fit is less clean than a vehicle-purchase audience.

**Decision:** Valuable but higher editorial and originality risk.

### 4. Used laptop/phone diagnostics

**For:** Strong connected searches such as “laptop battery health check” and substantial API viewing. [V09; T01–T05] Easy faceless screen-recording production and obvious affiliate categories.

**Against:** The search sample drifted toward smartphones and non-U.S. audiences. Product cycles are fast, incumbents are strong and hardware access is often required for credible demonstrations.

**Decision:** Commercial and producible, but crowded and less differentiated.

### 5. Airport transfer and layover navigation

**For:** High-stakes traveler intent, strong recent viewing and affiliate possibilities. [P01–P05; V07]

**Against:** Terminal layouts, entry rules, transit fares and construction change quickly. The best videos use current original airport footage. Asset-only execution risks becoming a static slideshow or stale map reading.

**Decision:** Good audience, poor guardrail fit.

### 6. Heat-pump/home-energy troubleshooting

**For:** Connected search terms showed demand with moderate competition; recent videos attracted hundreds of thousands of views. [V08; H01–H04] High advertiser value.

**Against:** Safety, licensing and liability are material. Credible troubleshooting often requires meters, equipment and filmed demonstrations. A faceless asset-only channel could explain systems but should not instruct dangerous repairs.

**Decision:** Attractive economics, unacceptable production/safety compromise for this brief.

### 7. Subscription traps and dark patterns

**For:** vidIQ showed promising connected keyword metrics and the format is easy to make with screen recordings and document analysis. [V10]

**Against:** The recent English long-form API sample was extremely weak—153 to 966 views—despite apparent keyword demand. [D01–D05] This is a direct evidence conflict. It may work as a series inside a consumer channel, but it is not yet a validated standalone engine.

**Decision:** Rejected because viewing behavior did not align with keyword demand.

### 8. Appliance error-code diagnosis

**For:** Huge problem-driven viewing; several sampled videos exceeded 1 million views. [A01–A05]

**Against:** The winning videos visibly demonstrate a real repair. Manufacturer-specific safety warnings and electrical/gas/water risks are substantial. Diagrams alone would be less useful and less trustworthy.

**Decision:** Demand validated, guardrail fit failed.

### 9. Sleeper-train planning

**For:** Strong connected rail terms and successful faceless channels. [V04; R01–R04]

**Against:** The channels proving the opportunity typically record the journeys themselves. Schedules, rolling stock and fares change. Licensed stock cannot replace the trust generated by an actual trip report.

**Decision:** Rejected because custom travel footage is a structural advantage.

### 10. Megaproject map explainers

**For:** Massive demand and viewing. [V06; M01–M05] Maps and diagrams are naturally faceless.

**Against:** The competitive bar includes world-class animation and documentary teams. Research, rights clearance and editing are expensive. A low-budget stock-footage version would risk inauthentic-content problems under YouTube’s policy. [S05]

**Decision:** Audience is large, but attainable competition and production simplicity are weak.

## Winner stress test before selection

The selected idea had to answer “yes” to all six gates:

1. **Are people actively searching across a cluster?** Yes, directionally, in vidIQ. [V01]
2. **Are they watching recent long-form videos?** Yes, across five sampled creators/topics. [Y01–Y05]
3. **Can a smaller faceless channel compete?** Adjacent channel data says yes, although no outcome is guaranteed.
4. **Can 100 episodes be generated without stretching the promise?** Yes: model-year files, battery tests, charging fit, warranty, recalls, depreciation, listing audits and total-cost comparisons.
5. **Is there commercial intent without requiring deceptive claims?** Yes: viewers are evaluating a large purchase and adjacent services.
6. **Can every episode add original value?** Yes, through a proprietary evidence hierarchy, risk score, decision tree and calculation—not through clip aggregation.

## Reserve plan

If the 90-day test fails, do not immediately abandon the research stack. The first pivot is **EV ownership diagnostics for existing owners**, using the same battery, charging and warranty expertise. The second, unrelated reserve niche is **home Wi-Fi diagnosis**, the runner-up in this tournament.
