# Brand Guide — EV Second Opinion

## Brand idea

**A calm diagnostic desk for a noisy purchase.** The brand should feel closer to a trusted inspection report than an enthusiast fan page or alarmist exposé.

## Core attributes

- Evidence-first
- Independent
- Calm under uncertainty
- Buyer-sided
- Practical
- Current
- Precise without pretending certainty

## Name and lockup

Primary name: **EV Second Opinion**

Primary lockup:

```
[Battery + magnifier mark] EV SECOND OPINION
                           KNOW BEFORE YOU BUY
```

Use “EVSO” only as an internal file prefix, never as the public-facing name until the full name has established recognition.

## Logo concept

The mark combines:

- a horizontal battery outline;
- a magnifying-glass handle at the lower-right;
- one missing battery bar, implying inspection rather than blind “full charge” confidence;
- a small check/risk dot that can switch between mint, amber and red in episode graphics.

A scalable concept is included at [assets/logo-concept.svg](../assets/logo-concept.svg).

## Color system

| Role | Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary dark | Diagnostic Navy | `#0B1623` | backgrounds, headers, authority |
| Secondary dark | Graphite | `#1E2935` | cards, chart panels |
| Trust accent | Battery Mint | `#32D6A0` | verified, buy, positive evidence |
| Caution accent | Signal Amber | `#FFB547` | unknown, wait, caution |
| Risk accent | Brake Red | `#F05B61` | walk, safety, failed gate |
| Light | Paper White | `#F4F7F8` | text and clean report surfaces |
| Muted | Steel | `#8EA0AF` | secondary labels and gridlines |

Accessibility:

- Never communicate verdict by color alone; pair color with **BUY**, **WAIT**, **WALK** and an icon.
- Use Paper White on Diagnostic Navy for body text.
- Use dark text on Battery Mint and Signal Amber.
- Do not set small white type on Battery Mint.

## Typography

Use widely available, license-safe families:

- Headlines: **Inter ExtraBold** or **Arial Bold** fallback.
- Body: **Inter Regular/Medium** or **Arial** fallback.
- Numbers/data: **Roboto Mono** or a system monospace fallback.

Rules:

- Sentence case in videos; all caps only for short verdict labels.
- Thumbnail text: maximum four words, ideally two or three.
- Never use condensed type for small data.
- Display prices and percentages in tabular/monospace numerals where possible.

## Voice

### Sounds like

- “That number is useful, but it does not prove what the seller says it proves.”
- “Here is what we can verify, and here is what still needs the exact VIN.”
- “This car is not a bad EV. It is a bad fit for this buyer at this price.”
- “The right answer changes when home charging disappears.”

### Does not sound like

- “This EV will bankrupt you.”
- “The battery is basically immortal.”
- “Dealers don’t want you to know this one secret.”
- “You’d be crazy to buy anything else.”
- “Guaranteed savings.”

## Editorial vocabulary

Preferred:

- evidence
- verified
- observed
- unknown
- conditional
- buyer fit
- remaining capability
- open recall
- warranty threshold
- dated listing sample
- total-cost scenario

Avoid unless directly sourced:

- defect epidemic
- lemon
- scam
- doomed
- bulletproof
- lifetime battery
- free charging
- maintenance-free
- investment

## Thumbnail system

A thumbnail must communicate **object + risk + verdict** at phone size.

### Layout

- Left 58%: one clean vehicle cutout or original silhouette/diagram.
- Right 42%: large verdict phrase.
- Top-left: small battery/magnifier brand mark.
- Bottom: thin score strip, e.g. `BATTERY 18/25  •  CHARGING 14/20`.
- Background: Diagnostic Navy with one angled technical-grid motif.

### Approved text archetypes

- BUY / WAIT / WALK
- BATTERY CLUE
- RANGE LIE?
- CREDIT ENDED
- CHECK THIS VIN
- WARRANTY TRAP?
- NO HOME CHARGER?
- PRICE DROP: WHY

Question marks are used only when the video genuinely resolves the question. Never use fake red arrows or shocked faces.

### Thumbnail test

At 10% scale, a stranger should identify:

1. a vehicle or battery subject;
2. one risk/decision;
3. the brand’s mint/amber/red verdict language.

A reusable SVG composition is included at [assets/thumbnail-template.svg](../assets/thumbnail-template.svg).

## Motion language

- Document scan: source page slides in, critical line highlights.
- Evidence stamp: VERIFIED / OBSERVED / UNKNOWN.
- Score build: numbers animate only after supporting evidence appears.
- Risk gate: a horizontal line stops when a non-negotiable gate fails.
- Comparison matrix: columns reveal one at a time; no decorative 3D charts.

Transitions are 6–12 frames and quiet. Avoid glitch effects, sparks, fake warning sirens and overused HUD overlays.

## Music and sound

- Minimal, restrained pulse under hooks and transitions.
- No music under dense warranty or tax language.
- One soft confirmation sound for verified evidence.
- One low, non-alarmist tone for a failed gate.
- Loudness and mix should prioritize narration intelligibility.

Use only properly licensed tracks and retain license records in the episode folder.

## Narration

Preferred options:

1. a consistent contracted narrator with commercial rights;
2. a licensed synthetic voice with a natural cadence and clear commercial terms;
3. the owner’s voice recorded without camera, if available later.

Do not clone a real person’s voice. Do not use a breathless “AI news” cadence. Every script receives a human read-through for pronunciation, emphasis and uncertainty language.

## On-screen source treatment

Every factual card includes a compact source footer:

`IRS • last verified 2026-07-12`

or

`YouTube Data API snapshot • 2026-07-12`

Official sources use a document icon. Market sources use a chart icon. Manufacturer terms use a wrench/document icon. Inference cards are explicitly labeled **EVSO inference**.

## Chart rules

- Start axes at zero unless the visual explicitly marks a truncated axis.
- Display sample size and date.
- Never imply connector metrics are official YouTube search volume.
- Use medians when outliers would distort an episode comparison.
- Highlight no more than one data series per screen.
- Put assumptions directly beneath cost calculations.

## Image and footage rules

Allowed:

- public-domain government imagery;
- manufacturer press images where the license/use permits and the image is used for commentary;
- licensed stock;
- original SVG diagrams, charts and animations;
- brief annotated screenshots necessary to explain a source or listing;
- screen recordings made for the episode.

Not allowed:

- downloaded owner footage used as visual filler;
- crash/fire compilations;
- dealership walkaround footage copied without meaningful transformation;
- a slideshow of press photos under generic narration;
- unlicensed music or logos presented as endorsement.

## Brand QA checklist

Before export:

- [ ] Title promises a decision, not vague information.
- [ ] Thumbnail has four words or fewer.
- [ ] Verified / observed / unknown labels are correct.
- [ ] Score is not shown before evidence.
- [ ] Every current claim has a last-verified date.
- [ ] No sponsor appears inside the verdict panel.
- [ ] No color-only communication.
- [ ] Narration distinguishes fact from inference.
- [ ] End screen points to the next decision in the playlist.

## Brand extension rules

Future newsletters, downloads or tools use the same evidence labels and scoring language. Do not create unrelated “EV lifestyle” merchandise. The brand is a decision service, not a fandom identity.
