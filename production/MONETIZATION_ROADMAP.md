# Monetization Roadmap

## Principle

Trust is the revenue asset. A used-EV buyer may be making a five-figure decision. Any monetization that weakens the independence of the verdict is strategically expensive.

No revenue, CPM, sponsor rate or time-to-monetization is guaranteed in this plan.

## YouTube policy fit

YouTube says monetized channels should be original and authentic, not mass-produced, repetitive or minimally transformed. [S05] EV Second Opinion is designed around original scripts, calculations, diagrams, source walkthroughs and model-specific verdicts. Licensed stock is context, not the product.

## Stage 0 — before meaningful audience

Goal: create evidence of buyer intent, not maximize links.

Allowed:

- a neutral “tools/resources mentioned” section;
- one clearly disclosed affiliate link when a product is genuinely demonstrated or evaluated;
- a free copyable checklist hosted later by the owner;
- YouTube’s built-in monetization when eligibility is reached.

Not allowed:

- ten unrelated accessory links under every upload;
- sponsored rankings;
- referral links disguised as sources;
- recommending diagnostic hardware as proof of accuracy without testing compatibility;
- warranty or insurance claims based on commission.

## Stage 1 — audience-supported platform revenue

Potential streams after platform eligibility:

- watch-page advertising;
- YouTube Premium revenue share;
- fan funding or memberships where available;
- channel membership with monthly evidence-file updates.

Membership value must be original and sustainable:

- monthly model-request vote;
- updated source/recall digest;
- live audio-only buyer clinic with no individualized mechanical/legal advice;
- downloadable comparison worksheets.

Do not put critical safety corrections behind a paywall.

## Stage 2 — affiliate categories

These are categories to investigate, not confirmed partnerships.

### Strong fit

- vehicle-history reports;
- home charging equipment and accessories;
- charging adapters with verified vehicle/network compatibility;
- tire inflators, tread gauges and ordinary inspection tools;
- EV route-planning or charging apps with affiliate programs;
- insurance or financing comparison services where disclosures are clear;
- automotive marketplaces.

### Conditional fit

- OBD readers and battery-report tools: only after hands-on compatibility validation and clear limits;
- extended warranty/service-contract providers: only after contract exclusions are reviewed;
- portable charging products: only with safety certification and manufacturer compatibility checks;
- home electrical products: never presented as DIY installation instructions.

### Poor fit

- speculative EV investments;
- unverified battery “reconditioning” products;
- products that bypass charging or safety controls;
- “guaranteed battery health” services without a defensible methodology;
- generic dropship accessories;
- pay-to-rank marketplaces.

## Stage 3 — sponsorship targets

Target **categories**, then screen individual companies.

- charging hardware manufacturers;
- vehicle-history and inspection services;
- used-EV marketplaces;
- insurance comparison platforms;
- route-planning/charging software;
- tire brands and tire retailers;
- home-energy and solar services;
- consumer finance education tools;
- data providers serving EV shoppers.

A target is not evidence that a company will sponsor the channel.

## Sponsor acceptance test

A sponsor must pass all eight:

1. Product is relevant to a buyer decision.
2. Claims can be independently verified.
3. Commercial terms allow honest criticism.
4. Sponsor cannot review or change the verdict.
5. No exclusivity blocks accurate comparisons.
6. Data collection and privacy practices are explainable.
7. Refund/cancellation terms are visible.
8. The product does not encourage unsafe high-voltage work.

Reject the deal if any gate fails.

## Sponsor placement

- No sponsor before the core problem is clear.
- Sponsor segment is visually separated and labeled.
- Sponsor does not appear in the score graphic.
- The video states whether the product was supplied or paid for.
- A negative or conditional verdict remains publishable.
- Source links are separated from affiliate links in the description.

## Paid verdict prohibition

Use this standard response in future negotiations:

> EV Second Opinion does not sell rankings, scores or verdicts. A sponsor may fund an episode or demonstrate a relevant tool, but it cannot select the conclusion, suppress material limitations or approve the editorial section.

## Digital products

### Free: Used EV Evidence Sheet

One-page checklist to drive repeat use and email opt-in later:

- VIN/recall check;
- exact trim and battery;
- warranty remaining;
- battery evidence provided;
- charging fit;
- insurance quote;
- tire/12-volt battery condition;
- out-the-door cost;
- unknowns and next action.

### Paid, only after audience validation

- model-specific buyer dossiers with last-verified dates;
- spreadsheet total-cost calculator;
- annual used-EV comparison database;
- structured course on used-EV due diligence.

A paid product should not merely repackage public source links. Its value must be organization, calculations, updates and decision clarity.

## Service opportunities

Possible later services:

- sponsored but independent market research;
- licensing original charts/animations;
- white-label buyer education for publishers;
- speaking or training on evidence-based EV shopping.

Avoid individualized vehicle inspection unless qualified professionals and appropriate insurance are involved.

## Monetization sequence

### Months 0–3

- Publish ten videos.
- Use zero or minimal affiliate links.
- Measure which decisions attract buyers.
- Build the free checklist structure.

### Months 4–6

- Add only the top one or two relevant affiliate categories.
- Create a sponsor one-sheet from actual audience data.
- Test a monthly source-update membership only if returning viewers exist.

### Months 7–12

- Pursue category sponsors aligned with the best-performing series.
- Build the first paid dossier only after viewers repeatedly request deeper files.
- Diversify so no sponsor category provides a controlling share of revenue.

The time ranges are planning stages, not performance promises.

## Sponsor one-sheet fields

Do not invent numbers. Populate from YouTube Analytics:

- 90-day views and watch hours;
- U.S. audience share;
- age bands;
- returning viewers;
- average percentage viewed;
- comments expressing active purchase intent;
- top model interests;
- click performance from disclosed links;
- editorial policy and prohibited deal types.

## Affiliate disclosure template

> Some links may be affiliate links. The channel may earn a commission at no extra cost to you. Links do not affect the EV Second Opinion score or verdict. Compatibility, pricing and terms can change; verify them for your exact vehicle and location.

## Conflict register

Maintain a public section in each description and an internal table:

| Company | Relationship | Products supplied | Cash paid | Affiliate | Episode | Verdict affected? |
|---|---|---|---:|---:|---|---|

The final column must always be **No**. If that cannot be stated truthfully, do not publish the deal.

## Revenue health metrics

Track:

- revenue per 1,000 views by stream;
- sponsor concentration;
- affiliate conversion by relevant video, not channel-wide spam;
- refunds/complaints related to recommended products;
- unsubscribe or retention change around sponsor segments;
- corrections involving commercial partners.

Optimize for lifetime trust, not maximum revenue on one upload.

## Monetization red lines

- paid positive reviews;
- undisclosed affiliate links;
- guaranteed savings or resale value;
- tax/legal claims from a sponsor script;
- unsafe charging or repair equipment;
- crypto/investment tie-ins based only on “EV audience”;
- fake scarcity countdowns;
- selling viewer data;
- withholding source links to protect a product sale.
