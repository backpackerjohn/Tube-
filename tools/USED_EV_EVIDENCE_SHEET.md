## EV Second Opinion score

This is an editorial framework, not a scientific certificate.

| Category | Max | Score | Evidence required |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Battery evidence | 25 |  | Pack identity, recall/replacement history, available diagnostic evidence, age/mileage context |
| Charging fit | 20 |  | Connector, home/work access, common routes, backup station, local price |
| Warranty protection | 15 |  | Exact vehicle-supplied/model-year terms, remaining time/miles, threshold, transferability |
| Safety and recall status | 15 |  | VIN lookup, completion records, known limits |
| Ownership-cost resilience | 15 |  | Insurance quote, charging, registration, tires, likely wear items |
| Price/value evidence | 10 |  | Dated comparable listings with geography, trim, mileage and fees |
| **Total** | **100** |  |  |

Planning bands:

- **80–100 — Buy candidate:** continue only after all vehicle-specific gates pass.
- **65–79 — Conditional buy:** price or use case must compensate for a known weakness.
- **50–64 — Wait / negotiate:** evidence or value is insufficient.
- **Below 50 — Walk for this use case:** not a universal judgment on the model.

## Seller questions

1. What is the exact VIN, trim and battery variant?
2. What is the original in-service date?
3. Has the high-voltage battery been repaired or replaced? Provide records.
4. Are all safety recalls completed? Provide records, not only a clean lookup screenshot.
5. What battery-health evidence can be supplied or independently obtained?
6. Are all charging cables, adapters and software-linked features included and transferable?
7. Will the seller permit an independent inspection and diagnostic scan?
8. What is the itemized out-the-door price with every optional product removed?
9. What material defect, warning light, charging issue or feature limitation is known?
10. What return, arbitration or warranty terms apply in writing?
